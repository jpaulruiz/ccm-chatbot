The problem

Chatbots are sprawling — and nobody owns the whole picture.

Fragmented

Every site ships its own widget, its own prompt, its own model. No shared standard.

Blind

No single view of volume, resolution or where bots are failing. Issues surface from customers, not dashboards.

Slow to ship

Spinning up a new agent means engineering time every single time. Ideas die in backlogs.

Result: rising support cost, inconsistent brand voice, and AI spend nobody can fully account for.

The solution

CCM HQ — the management layer for AI agents.

Build an agent once. Embed it on any site. Watch every conversation in one dashboard. Govern model, brand and budget centrally.

  • ✓ Build agents from templates — sales concierge, support triage, docs answerer, lead qualifier.
  • ✓ Deploy with one embed snippet; assign which agents respond on which site.
  • ✓ Measure volume, resolution, intents and handoffs per site and per agent.
  • ✓ Govern default model, brand color, time zone and team from one workspace.

How it works

From zero to live agent in four steps.

1

Pick a template

Start from a preconfigured agent — prompt, tools and model already wired.

2

Connect tools

Give it Slack handoff, a webhook stream, or read-only DB lookups.

3

Drop the embed

Paste one snippet on any site, assign the agent, go live.

4

Measure & iterate

Watch resolution and intents; feed gaps back into the idea board.

Why us, not a widget

Central governance is the moat.

One model policy

Set a default model workspace-wide (e.g. gemini-3-flash). Control cost and quality in one place, not 12.

Consistent brand

Brand color, widget header and email handoffs centrally enforced across every site.

Embed health

See healthy / warning per domain before customers do.

Built-in feedback loop

Ideas board turns customer chat gaps directly into a ranked, shippable roadmap.

The ask

Greenlight a focused pilot.

Working prototype already exists. Next step is a real pilot on a handful of our own properties to validate resolution lift and cost savings.

  • 1. Approve a 1-team pilot across 3–5 internal sites.
  • 2. Pick a cost baseline so we can prove support-ticket deflection.
  • 3. 30-day readout: resolution %, handoff rate, cost per resolved chat.
